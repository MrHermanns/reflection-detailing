What's the difference between a wax, a sealant, and a ceramic coating? +
  • Wax — 1-3 months. Warm, deep shine. Easiest to apply, shortest protection.
  • Spray Sealants — 3-6 months. Easier than wax to apply, better durability.
  • Ceramic Coatings — 2-5+ years. Semi-permanent hydrophobic layer. Strongest protection against UV, bird droppings, tree sap, and road contaminants.

Ceramic is the best long-term value if you're keeping the car more than 2 years.

What is paint correction, and do I need it? +

Paint correction is a machine-polishing process that removes a micro-thin layer of clear coat to eliminate swirl marks, light scratches, water spots, and oxidation.

You need it if:

  • Your paint looks "hazy" in direct sunlight
  • You can see spider-web swirls under a streetlight
  • You're about to apply a ceramic coating

A 1-step correction removes most defects; a 2-step handles heavier damage.

How often should I have my car detailed? +

For daily drivers in the San Diego climate, we recommend a Mini Detail every 4-6 weeks and a Full Detail every 3-4 months. Coastal vehicles (Imperial Beach, Coronado) benefit from more frequent exterior work due to salt air. Our monthly maintenance plan starts at $149/mo and saves about $30/mo versus one-off pricing.

Can you remove pet hair, stains, and odors? +

Yes. Pet hair removal is a $40 add-on to any interior package — we use specialized rubber extractors for embedded hair. Stains and odors are addressed with a hot-water extractor and enzymatic treatments. Severe cases (vomit, smoke, flood) may require a two-visit process.
